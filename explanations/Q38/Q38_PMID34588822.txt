How to heal a pressure wound on the ear?

Pressure injury (PI) corresponds to a skin damage of ischemic aetiology that affects the integrity of the skin and is produced by prolonged pressure or friction between a hard internal and external surface.
Treatment can be challenging when there is no resolution with usual care.
The use of autologous platelet-rich plasma (APRP) gel arises as a therapeutic possibility in the presence of chronic pressure injuries.
The case of a patient with chronic PI who has been treated with APRP is presented, achieving resolution of the lesion.
