How is diabetes diagnosed?

Diabetes mellitus is a common disease whose side effects are severe.
For decades, the diagnosis of diabetes and prediabetes was using only fasting (without food) glucose or glucose two hours during an by-mouth (oral) glucose sensitivity test.
Recently, it is possible to use HbA1c (a simple blood sugar test).
Each of these tests has pros and cons that must be well known by clinicians for better care for patients.
So they could use one, two or three of this tests to reach a proper diagnosis.
This article's aim is about the strong and weak points of these tests.
