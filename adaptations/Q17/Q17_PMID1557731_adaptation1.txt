How do you assist an unconscious victim who is already vomiting?

The most important thing in the emergency care of patients is making sure air passes freely.
Removing blocked tissue, loosening objects that shouldn't be swallowed and putting the patient in the right position usually open airways and allow breathing in injured people.
If breathing has stopped, a breathing tube is needed and should be given by a trained professional.
Correct control of airways may decrease illness and death.
The author discusses control of airways and normal breathing in real life situations.
